# Zari360 Connect - Positioning and Messaging Guide

**Date:** 2026-06-01
**Use this for:** every piece of Connect marketing copy (landing, product pages, ads, WhatsApp, Instagram, emails, decks). It is the single source of truth for what we say and how we say it.

This guide is grounded in a verified deep-research pass (22 confirmed findings, 3 refuted and discarded). Research synthesis is in section 6.

---

## 1. One-line positioning

> **Zari360 Connect is the textile trade's own network, marketplace, and hiring hub.**

Connect is where the whole textile industry meets, sells, hires, and stays in touch - LinkedIn, IndiaMART, and a hiring portal, but built only for textiles and the way this trade actually does business.

## 2. The three layers (always describe Connect as these three jobs)

1. **Network and presence** - be seen by the whole trade. Profile, company page, storefront, feed.
2. **Marketplace** - find suppliers and buyers, deal directly. Enquiries and quote requests, no middleman, no checkout.
3. **Jobs and hiring** - hire skilled people or find your next job, with verified employers.

**Also served (now live): training institutes and their students.** A training institute or academy can open a free page, list its courses, and place its students; a student can carry an institute-confirmed "Trained at" credential into the trade (enquire to enrol, no checkout). Keep this secondary to the three core layers, as a feeder into hiring. See `segment-institutes-and-adjacent-users.md`.

## 3. The four messages that win (in priority order)

1. **Genuine, verified connections - not a wall of fake leads.** This is our sharpest message. The category leader is widely distrusted because one enquiry gets blasted to 10-15 sellers and many "leads" are fake or other suppliers. Connect promises real, relevant, verified businesses. Lead with this wherever a buyer or seller is the audience.
2. **Verified on Zari360 (the moat).** Many businesses here also run their daily operations on Zari360 ERP, so they are real, active, and accountable. This is operational truth no competitor can fake. Surface it as a badge and a trust line.
3. **Close the deal on WhatsApp.** Every enquiry and chat can continue on WhatsApp in one tap. We capture the lead; the trade closes the way it always has.
4. **Free to start, in your language, on your phone.** Free to begin, full Gujarati / Hindi / English, mobile and web. Removes every reason to hesitate at signup.

## 4. Voice and tone

- **Plain India-English.** Short sentences. Outcome first, feature second. Write the way a Surat trader would explain it to another trader.
- **Trade-role-neutral.** Manufacturers, workshops, processing units, suppliers, wholesalers, buyers, designers, professionals. Use "karigar" only sparingly, where local trust genuinely helps, never as the headline frame.
- **Confident, not boastful.** Claim category leadership for textiles and let trust signals do the bragging. Avoid hype words ("revolutionary", "world-class").
- **Outcome-led hooks.** Echo the "business gets done here" / "kaam yahin banta hai" energy without copying the line. Lead with what the user gets.

## 5. Terminology do and don't

| Do                                                                         | Don't                                       |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Enquire for pricing, Send enquiry                                          | Request a quote (US-style), Buy now         |
| Quote request (RFQ)                                                        | Purchase order, Checkout                    |
| PIN code                                                                   | ZIP code                                    |
| Verified on Zari360, Verified business                                     | Trusted partner (vague)                     |
| Textile trade, the trade                                                   | Just "embroidery" as the whole market       |
| Manufacturers, workshops, processing units, suppliers, wholesalers, buyers | Karigar/owner as the default audience frame |
| Continue on WhatsApp                                                       | Contact us (generic)                        |
| Free to start                                                              | Free forever (overpromise; it is freemium)  |

**Hard rules:**

- No em-dashes anywhere. Use a hyphen, comma, or period. (Project-wide ban.)
- Never promise leads are guaranteed. Paid plans boost visibility, they do not guarantee deals.
- Reviews and ratings are shown only after a real deal is confirmed by both sides. Never imply open review-bombing.
- Cite competitor weaknesses by describing the better experience we offer, not by naming and attacking competitors in public copy.

## 6. Research synthesis (verified, with confidence)

- **Lead quality is the single biggest conversion wedge (high).** The category leader is criticized for unverified, non-serious inquiries blasted to many sellers; a textile vertical wins by promising verified buyers and genuine, relevant leads. Cite as a positioning opportunity, not a hard market statistic.
- **Lead with category leadership and outcomes, not features (high).** Anchor on "the textile industry's own network and marketplace" and a vernacular, outcome-led hook.
- **Trust stack = verification badges + ratings/reviews + rich profile/catalog pages (high).** Add our unique "Verified on Zari360 / ERP-backed" signal on top.
- **WhatsApp-first, image and video-driven (high).** Surat textile traders already negotiate over WhatsApp with images and videos.
- **Multilingual gu/hi/en, mobile-first, vernacular search (medium).** Roughly two-thirds of Indian users prefer native-language content.
- **Free-to-start with visible lead tracking is the baseline (high).** Freemium where paid boosts visibility but never guarantees leads.
- **Segment to real Surat trade roles (high):** weaving/power-loom units, processing units (dyeing/printing/embroidery), traders/wholesalers. Designers are a weakly-evidenced segment - keep secondary.
- **Jobs layer = Apna-style (high):** vernacular worker identity plus verified employers so hiring feels moderated and safe.
- **India terminology in CTAs (medium):** "Enquire for pricing", "PIN code". Treat "Request a call-back vs Schedule a demo" as an A/B test, not a fact.

**Refuted and not to be used:** specific IndiaMART scale numbers ("60 million buyers / est. 1996"); "vernacular availability" as the single hook; "verification" as the one central headline value prop. Verification supports the wedge, it is not the whole story.

**Open questions to validate later:** real demand for a designer segment; call-back vs demo conversion; which layer is the acquisition wedge vs the retention hook; which verification mechanics (GST/PAN/Udyam) traders will trust and pay for.
