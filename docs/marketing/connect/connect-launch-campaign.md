# Zari360 Connect - Launch Campaign Plan

**Date:** 2026-06-01
**Depends on:** `connect-positioning-and-messaging.md` (voice and messages), `2026-06-01-connect-marketing-rebuild-design.md` (the pages).
**Read this as:** the go-to-market plan to drive registrations to Connect once the rebuilt pages are live. It is a reusable template; update dates and numbers each campaign.

---

## 1. Objective

Drive qualified registrations to Zari360 Connect from the Surat and Gujarat textile trade, then activate them (complete a profile or list a product or post a job) within the first week.

**Primary metric:** new Connect registrations.
**Activation metric:** percent of new users who complete one core action (profile, listing, company page, job post, or enquiry) within 7 days.
**Quality metric:** percent of marketplace enquiries marked genuine by the receiving business.

## 2. Audiences (priority order)

1. **Suppliers and wholesalers** (fabric, yarn, machinery, raw material) - most to gain from genuine leads; strongest WhatsApp behaviour.
2. **Manufacturers and workshops** (weaving, power-loom, garment) - both buy material and hire.
3. **Processing units** (dyeing, printing, embroidery, zari) - our credibility base; warm audience.
4. **Buyers and sourcing** - pulled in by the supplier base.
5. **Designers and professionals** (secondary) - validate demand before heavy spend.

## 3. Positioning per audience (one line each)

- Suppliers: "List once, get genuine enquiries from real buyers. Close on WhatsApp."
- Manufacturers: "Find verified material suppliers and hire skilled people, in one place."
- Processing units: "Show your work to the whole trade and win more job work."
- Buyers: "Source from verified textile businesses, not a wall of fake listings."
- Designers/professionals: "Showcase your work and get found by the trade."

## 4. Channels

- **WhatsApp (primary).** Broadcast and one-to-one to existing ERP users and trade contacts. Short, vernacular, image-led. Every message ends with a one-tap link to register or to a storefront. This is the highest-leverage channel for this trade.
- **Instagram and Reels.** Visual-first: real product showcases, "before/after" work, short founder explainers in Gujarati/Hindi. Strong for processing units and designers.
- **Trade associations and markets.** Surat textile market associations, weavers' and processors' bodies, mandi/market WhatsApp groups. On-ground QR codes for storefronts (the product already generates packaging QR codes).
- **Referral loop.** "Invite a supplier you trust." Verified businesses inviting verified businesses reinforces the trust wedge.
- **Local language SEO.** The rebuilt `/connect` page targets "textile marketplace", "textile jobs", "textile suppliers Surat" intent. Phase 2 sub-pages extend this.

## 5. Message arc (the funnel)

1. **Hook (awareness):** the wedge - genuine, verified connections, not fake leads.
2. **Proof (consideration):** Verified on Zari360, real businesses, work showcases, WhatsApp close.
3. **Reassurance (decision):** free to start, your language, your phone, no card.
4. **Action:** register, then complete one core action. WhatsApp follow-up nudges activation.

## 7. Asset checklist

- Rebuilt `/connect` page and reframed landing (this initiative).
- WhatsApp broadcast copy in gu / hi / en (from the copy bank in the messaging doc).
- 5-8 Instagram/Reels scripts (vernacular, visual-first).
- Storefront QR cards for on-ground distribution.
- 2 short founder videos (Gujarati/Hindi): "what is Connect", "why verified matters".
- Referral message and link.

## 8. Guardrails

- Never promise guaranteed leads or sales. Free to start, not free forever.
- Keep enquiry quality high from day one; a flood of junk enquiries kills the wedge. Lightweight verification before a business can send bulk enquiries.
- Respect notification limits; do not spam. The platform that respects attention wins this market.
- All public copy follows the messaging guide, including the no-em-dash rule and the post-deal-only reviews rule.

## 9. Open questions to resolve before spend

- Which single layer is the acquisition wedge for cold audiences (likely WhatsApp-driven marketplace) vs the retention hook (likely network)?
- Designer-segment demand: validate with a small test before dedicated spend.
- Verification mechanics traders will trust and, later, pay for.
